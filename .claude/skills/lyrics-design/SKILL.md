# Lyrics Design Workflow

Design the concept and structure of a song through focused questions.

## Workflow

Make a todo list for all tasks in this workflow and work through them one by one.

### 1. Ask Core Questions
Before proposing anything, ask:
1. What is the emotional core — what feeling should the listener leave with?
2. Who is the speaker, and who or what are they addressing?
3. Any genre, tempo, language, or length constraints?

Do not propose structure until these three are answered.

### 2. Propose Structure
Based on answers, propose:
- Section breakdown (e.g. Verse / Pre-chorus / Chorus / Verse / Bridge / Outro)
- Thematic arc per section — what emotional or narrative shift happens in each
- A key image or motif to anchor the song's identity

### 3. Confirm and Refine
Present as a proposal, not a final decision. Ask: does this match the intent?
Refine based on feedback before moving to writing.

### 4. Save Design
Save the confirmed structure to `lyrics/<song-name>/design.md`.
Create the directory if it doesn't exist.

## Wrap Up
State: what structure was finalized, what file was saved, whether to proceed directly to writing or pause here.
